experiments: focal loss 

Focal Loss (gamma=2)
The model was confident on excavator (loss 0.35) and very confident on train/train_tracks (0.04/0.1) — standard CE kept rewarding those easy correct predictions.
 Focal Loss multiplies each sample's loss by (1 − p_t)², so easy correct predictions contribute almost nothing to the gradient and the 13 confused workers
  samples get ~20× more training signal relative to the easy ones.

Crop scale (0.5→0.2, 1.0)
At scale=0.5 minimum, the model never sees an object occupying less than ~50% of the image area during training, but at inference the tiny workers 
in wide-angle shots might occupy 5-10%. Dropping to 0.2 means the model is explicitly trained on scenes where the target is small — it learns to distinguish 
"worker standing next to a train" vs "just a train" at realistic scale.

What to watch: the workers F1 score specifically (not overall accuracy). If it goes from ~0.90 to ~0.95+ the fix is working. 
If workers→train confusion persists after retraining, the next step would be hard-negative mining or collecting more workers-near-train training images.

Why only stages 2 & 3?
ConvNeXt has 4 stages. Stages 0–1 learn low-level edges/textures (universal, no need to touch them). Stages 2–3 learn semantic mid/high-level features — exactly where "worker holding a shovel near an excavator" vs "just an excavator" gets encoded. Unfreezing only these keeps training stable and avoids catastrophic forgetting of the ImageNet features.

Why lr=1e-5 (100× lower)?
The backbone weights are already well-trained on ImageNet. A high LR would destroy them. At 1e-5 you're nudging the representations to distinguish your specific confusion cases, not relearning from scratch.

Why batch_size=8?
More parameters are now accumulating gradients — halving the batch keeps you within VRAM budget. On an H100 you likely have headroom, but 8 is safe.

What to expect: the excavator↔workers confusion (13 cases total) should drop significantly because the backbone will learn that "worker pixels" have human-shaped features even when surrounded by excavator context.
